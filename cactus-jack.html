Jacques Berman Webster II · Houston, TX

Born April 30, 1991 in Houston, Texas — Travis Scott launched Cactus Jack Records on January 10, 2017, backed by a deal with Epic Records and prior relationships with T.I.'s Grand Hustle and Kanye West's GOOD Music as a producer.

His mission was explicit: sign artists without the industry lies about budgets, release dates, or creative control. Every decision at Cactus Jack reflects Houston's independent spirit and Scott's obsession with full creative ownership.

"I'm not doing it to have financial control over my music. I want first and foremost to help other artists, launch new names, to provide opportunities."

Travis Scott — Numéro Magazine

6 Major Partnerships
2017 — Ongoing
Nike / Jordan Brand

The most coveted sneaker partnership in streetwear. Scott received his own signature Jordan silhouette — the Jumpman Jack — launching on his birthday, April 30, 2024. Multiple sold-out Air Jordan, Air Max, and Dunk collaborations over eight years.

2020
McDonald's

The Travis Scott Meal — the first celebrity meal deal at McDonald's since Michael Jordan in 1992. A cultural phenomenon that sold out Quarter Pounders across the U.S. and crashed the McDonald's app.

2021
Christian Dior

Debuted at Paris Men's Fashion Week, June 2021. The first time Dior partnered with a musician on a full menswear collection — and the first time the Dior logo was permitted to be altered. A landmark in fashion history.

2021
Cactus Farm

A licensed cannabis cultivation venture launched under the Cactus Jack brand — Scott extending his creative identity into wellness and lifestyle verticals.

2023
A24 Films

Partnership with prestige studio A24 to release Circus Maximus, a visual companion film to the Utopia album era — Travis bringing his cinematic vision into feature-length form.

2024
Jordan Jumpman Jack

Travis Scott's first fully owned signature sneaker silhouette — the Jumpman Jack TR. Launched April 30, 2024, on his 33rd birthday. A milestone marking his transition from collaborator to signature athlete.

Former · 2017–2019
Smokepurpp

Florida rapper and early Cactus Jack signee. His departure in 2019 was self-directed — Smokepurpp publicly clarified that Travis Scott did not drop him; he chose to pursue opportunities through Interscope and Alamo Records. Creative direction differences were reported as the catalyst.

Former · Brief 2021
Malu Trevejo

Cuban singer and social media personality who announced a signing, then departed within weeks. The exit was surrounded by public controversy and allegations that the deal had been "staged" — claims she denied. She later cited personal life changes, maintained a positive relationship with Scott, and moved on.
