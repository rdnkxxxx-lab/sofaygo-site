2001

The Beginning

Andre Dontrel Burt Jr. is born on October 3rd, 2001, in Grand Rapids, Michigan, the eldest of three brothers — Alijah and Alandre would follow. His household is deeply rooted in faith: his father is a pastor and his mother serves as an evangelist. Rhythm, community, and spiritual expression shape his earliest world.

After spending his first two years in Grand Rapids, the family relocates to Cobb County, Georgia — the northern suburbs of Atlanta — where Andre would spend his entire childhood and adolescence. The Atlanta music scene would eventually become the air he breathes.

2009

GarageBand
at Nine

In the fourth grade, Andre joins the school chorus — his first formal encounter with making music. He quickly takes things further. In his grandmother's basement, armed with nothing but a laptop and GarageBand, he records his very first song. He's nine years old.

The quality doesn't matter. What matters is the feeling — a kid discovering that something inside him sounds exactly right when it gets out of his head and into a track. Before rap, he was drawn to dancing and acting. Music would eventually win out, but those instincts for performance never left him.

2015

Finding the
Voice

Junior and senior year of high school — that's when it gets serious. While attending Etowah High School in Woodstock, Georgia, Andre starts rapping under the name Trvllinese. The name is strange, intentionally so — a signal that he doesn't plan to fit any existing mold.

Growing up moving between schools meant making friends was hard. Music filled that gap. During this era he links with rapper Lil Tecca, a creative partnership that would define his early sound. He also later turns down a college path entirely, choosing music full-time right after graduation.

"It was weird growing up in Atlanta because I moved schools a lot, so it was really hard for me to be around people and make friends. But I always loved music."

2018

Into the
Underground

Still performing as Trvllinese, Andre drops We Are Aliens and Goonland — two self-released EPs that circulate through SoundCloud and underground rap communities. Raw and unpolished, they show a developing melodic instinct that would become his calling card.

The projects don't reach mainstream ears yet. But they plant seeds. A growing cult following begins to take notice of the Atlanta kid with the alien stage name and the ability to make pain sound effortless.

2019

The Slow
Burn Begins

June 29th: debut mixtape War drops — eight tracks of melodic trap that earn him real recognition in the Atlanta underground. July 31st: "Knock Knock" arrives, produced with Lil Tecca, with a music video directed by Cole Bennett of Lyrical Lemonade.

The song doesn't blow up overnight — but it has staying power that few tracks do. This same year, Internet Money founder Taz Taylor offers him a deal. He declines. The move is bold for a still-emerging artist, but it signals something: Andre Burt Jr. is betting entirely on himself. The rebrand to SoFaygo makes it official.

2020

The Moment
Arrives

Late 2020: "Knock Knock" goes viral on TikTok. A song recorded a year prior suddenly soundtracks millions of videos. Travis Scott — still not officially connected — starts playing SoFaygo's music at his daughter Stormi's birthday party. Industry heads take notice.

The track earns RIAA Platinum certification. Meanwhile, SoFaygo drops two projects: Angelic 7 (January 10) — his first all-platform release — and After Me (December 17), a more polished tape featuring fan favourites "Off the Map" and "Everyday." The song "Everyday" gained massive attention and helped establish him as someone worth watching.

2021

Everything
Changes

February 2021. SoFaygo flies out West. He meets Travis Scott. He signs to Cactus Jack Records. The deal happens through a chain of relationships: his friend Jamie introduced him to co-manager Barry Hefner (manager of EarthGang and J.I.D), who connected him with Travis directly. Multiple labels were interested. He chose Cactus Jack.

June: he performs on Genius's Open Mic series. August 20: he appears on Trippie Redd's Trip at Knight on "MP5" — his Billboard Hot 100 debut at #86. September 1: he drops "Let's Lose Our Minds", his first Cactus Jack release and lead single for the forthcoming debut album. He also links with Travis on "Turks" — one of the year's standout moments.

"Really, Barry Hefner got us in contact. I met Barry through one of my mans, Jamie."

2022

Heart on
the Sleeve

Two EPs arrive before the album: B4Pink in June and the SoundCloud-exclusive Babyjack in July. Then November 11th: Pink Heartz drops on Cactus Jack / Epic Records — 16 tracks featuring Ken Carson, Lil Uzi Vert, and Pharrell Williams on "Took Off." HipHopDX names him their Rising Star of 2022. Apple Music crowns him their "Up Next" artist. He lands a Beats by Dre campaign alongside LeBron and Bronny James.

The album is his most personal work — built on vulnerability, emotional transparency, and the belief that wearing your heart on your sleeve is a strength, not a weakness. Producer Bakkwoods helps him unlock a new level of maturity.

"Having a pink heart means you wear your heart on your sleeve. You're very open about your feelings and who you are."

SoFaygo — Hypebeast (Nov 2022)

"I go through emotions and that's what I'm trying to capture in Pink Heartz. I'm not trying to capture just one emotion either — we're going through a roller coaster."

2023

Staying in
His World

June 14th: GO+ drops — a 12-track EP on Cactus Jack that expands his sonic vocabulary. The project gets covered by Lyrical Lemonade, where he talks about respecting the grind and his evolution as an artist.

Behind the scenes, 2023 is a year of building. Touring. Recording. A growing frustration begins to emerge — fans and press notice the gap between his profile and his output. But SoFaygo stays patient, insisting the right project takes the right time.

"I just want the world to remember Faygo for being Faygo, bro. Just being in his own world."

2024

War 2 On
the Horizon

SoFaygo re-emerges with "Precision" featuring Don Toliver — a spacey, high-energy track that re-announces his presence on the map. He follows with "Life So Crazy" and joins Lil Tecca's tour, playing to some of the biggest crowds of his career.

In an EARMILK interview, he reveals he's been drawing new inspiration from rock acts like The Ramones and Blondie, leaning into a "young rock star" ethos. He's working on a full-length sequel to his 2019 debut War — aiming for 16–18 tracks. He's also candid about the frustration: leaks have derailed multiple projects. Pressure, a SoundCloud-only EP, drops late in the year.

"I'm a young rock star. I want to give off that same vibe, but I want to do it my own way."

SoFaygo — EARMILK (Jul 2024)

2025

Locked In
at Last

July 2025: JackBoys 2 drops. SoFaygo appears on two tracks — his first official collaboration with Travis Scott since signing to Cactus Jack in 2021. The track "MM3" brings energy to his live shows and reconnects him with the mainstream audience he'd been building toward.

October 31st: he announces MANIA. November 21st: it lands on all platforms — 19 tracks with features from Kaash Paige and Hardrock. V Magazine covers him in a feature titled "Back in the Mix and Feeling the Love." After years of leaks, delays, and "what happened to SoFaygo?" narratives, the answer is clear — he never left. He was building.
